We build things that didn't exist yesterday.

Patty is a product company from Seoul. We are engineers, designers, and operators who spent years at places like Google and Amazon — building at scale, learning what ships and what doesn't, and eventually asking ourselves a harder question than any system design problem:

*What would we build if we stopped optimizing for someone else's roadmap?*

So we started. We ship fast, break assumptions, and obsess over the details that most teams skip. We care about craft. We care about the feeling when something just works — when you don't need a tutorial, when the interface disappears and only the outcome remains.

We're not chasing a trend. We're building a company that lasts — one that earns trust by shipping things people actually want, in a market that's honest enough to tell you when you're wrong.

Korea is where we started. It won't be where we stop.
